# STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
## Machinery Guarding and Fencing on Rotating Equipment

**Document ID:** HS-SOP-2019-041
**Revision:** v3.0
**Effective Date:** 12-Oct-2019
**Prepared By:** Ramesh K., Plant Safety Officer
**Approved By:** Plant Manager, Area 4
**Review Cycle:** 3 Years

---

### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish mandatory requirements for the installation, maintenance, and inspection of physical guards and fencing around all rotating and hazardous machinery within the plant. This ensures the prevention of accidental contact by personnel, thereby mitigating the risk of entanglement, crushing, or amputation.

### 2. Regulatory Basis
This SOP has been developed to ensure strict compliance with **The Factories Act, 1948, Section 21 (Fencing of machinery)**. Non-compliance with this procedure constitutes a regulatory violation and will be subject to disciplinary action.

### 3. Scope
This procedure applies to all operational areas, including but not limited to:
- Compressors (e.g., C-104, C-105)
- Pumps and motor assemblies
- Conveyor systems
- Turbines and prime movers

### 4. Responsibilities
- **Maintenance Department:** Responsible for fabricating, installing, and repairing all machine guards.
- **Shift Supervisors:** Ensure that no machinery is operated unless all guards are securely in place.
- **Operators:** Must visually inspect guards before starting equipment and immediately report any missing or damaged guards. Operators are strictly prohibited from bypassing or removing guards during normal operation.

### 5. Procedure for Guard Removal and Reinstallation
Guards may only be removed for maintenance or inspection under the following conditions:
1. **Lock-Out / Tag-Out (LOTO):** The equipment must be fully de-energized, isolated, and locked out according to SOP HS-SOP-2018-012.
2. **Permit to Work:** A valid Maintenance Permit must be issued by the Shift Supervisor.
3. **Reinstallation:** Upon completion of maintenance, the technician must reinstall all guards securely. The equipment MUST NOT be re-energized until the Shift Supervisor has verified the guards are correctly fitted.

### 6. Design and Construction of Guards
As per Factories Act Section 21 requirements, all guards must be:
- Of substantial construction (e.g., steel mesh, solid metal plating).
- Constantly maintained and kept in position.
- Designed such that it prevents any part of the human body from entering the danger zone.

### 7. Specific Instructions for Compressors (C-104, etc.)
- Ensure the coupling guards are tightly bolted.
- Flywheels and drive belts must be fully enclosed by a cage guard.
- Daily inspection logs must reflect the condition of the compressor fencing.
